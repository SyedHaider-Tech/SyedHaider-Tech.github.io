About

My career began in the hard constraints of the physical world. A PhD in Petroleum Engineering trained me to reason about multiphase flow, thermodynamics, and the brutal economics of moving energy at scale — a discipline where a rounding error in the physics becomes a nine-figure error on the balance sheet.

That foundation became a bridge. Over the last decade I moved from research into operations, managing multi-million-dollar upscaling projects and steering corporate technology portfolios where the mandate was never "is this clever?" but "does this survive first contact with a commercial P&L?"

Today I sit between the lab and the market. I run technical and commercial due diligence for investors, design scale-up strategy for founders, and advise C-suites on which deep-tech bets are real — separating genuine physics-backed advantage from narrative and subsidy dependence.

The through-line is simple: rigor over hype. Frontier innovation only matters if it can be built, financed, and deployed at the scale the world actually needs.
